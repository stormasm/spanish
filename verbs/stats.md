
Approximately 72% of Spanish verbs end in -ar, but they are the most regular verb group; less than 5% of all -ar verbs are irregular. While -ar verbs dominate total verb count, they constitute a minority of the irregular verbs when compared to the highly irregular -er and -ir categories.

Key statistics regarding irregular verb distribution:

- Irregular Distribution: Based on one dataset of over 1,200 verbs, -ar verbs make up about 72% of all verbs, but only a fraction are irregular. Another analysis suggests -ar verbs represent only ~13% of irregular verb models.
- Most Regularity: Almost all -ar verbs are regular.
- High Irregularity in -er/-ir: Conversely, over 72% of all -er verbs are irregular, and nearly half of all -ir verbs are irregular.

Although most -ar verbs are regular, some of the most frequently used verbs in Spanish, such as estar, dar, and andar, are irregular
