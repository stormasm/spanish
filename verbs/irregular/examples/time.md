

To tell time in Spanish, use "Es la" for 1:00 and "Son las" for all other hours. Add minutes using "y" (plus) up to 30, and subtract using "menos" (minus) for the remaining 30 minutes of the hour. \[[1](https://studyspanish.com/grammar/lessons/time), [2](https://www.spanishpod101.com/blog/2020/07/31/telling-time-in-spanish/), [3](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yspfWcVGwy4&t=583)\]

1\. The Basics

* ¿Qué hora es? (What time is it?)  
* Es la una. (It is one o'clock.)  
* Son las dos / tres / cinco... (It is two / three / five...) \[[1](https://www.enforex.com/language/vocabulary-time.html), [2](https://www.tuaprendesahora.com/clases/what-time-is-it/67), [3](https://studyspanish.com/grammar/lessons/time)\]

2\. Minutes (Up to 30\)

Say the hour, add "y", and state the minutes. \[[1](https://studyspanish.com/grammar/lessons/time)\]

* 3:05 \= Son las tres y cinco.  
* 4:10 \= Son las cuatro y diez.  
* 7:30 \= Son las siete y media (or treinta). \[[1](https://www.mondly.com/blog/tell-time-spanish-guide-beginners/), [2](https://academiajoseperaza.com/errores-fatales-al-aprender-las-horas-en-ingles/), [3](https://studyspanish.com/grammar/lessons/time), [4](https://www.spanishdict.com/guide/telling-time-in-spanish)\]

3\. Minutes (After 30\)

Instead of counting past 30, go to the next hour and subtract the minutes using "menos". \[[1](https://studyspanish.com/grammar/lessons/time), [2](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yspfWcVGwy4&t=583)\]

* 8:40 \= Son las nueve menos veinte (20 minutes to 9).  
* 1:50 \= Son las dos menos diez (10 minutes to 2). \[[1](https://www.spanishpodcast.net/tellingtimeinspanish/), [2](https://mhcc.pressbooks.pub/spa101/chapter/telling-time/), [3](https://app.wizer.me/category/spanish/WKTRGD-la-hora---telling-time-in-spanish), [4](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yspfWcVGwy4&t=583)\]

4\. Key Fractions

* 15 minutes past: y cuarto  
* 30 minutes past: y media  
* 15 minutes to: menos cuarto \[[1](https://www.bbc.co.uk/bitesize/articles/zmftwty), [2](https://commongroundinternational.com/spanish-for-educators/maestro-miercoles-how-to-talk-about-classroom-schedules-in-spanish/)\]

5\. Time of Day

Add these phrases to avoid confusion between a.m. and p.m.:

* In the morning: de la mañana  
* In the afternoon: de la tarde  
* At night: de la noche  
* Noon: del mediodía  
* Midnight: de la medianoche \[[1](https://www.spanishdict.com/guide/telling-time-in-spanish), [2](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mTFzoEWJuEE&t=52), [3](https://www.thoughtco.com/telling-time-in-spanish-3078120)\]
