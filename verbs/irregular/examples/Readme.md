### caer

caigo means I fall or I am falling.    
It is also commonly used in phrases meaning "I understand" or to indicate if you are liked by someone.

Here are a few examples:

- "Me caigo de sueño." (I'm falling asleep on my feet/I'm exhausted.)
- "Casi me caigo de la silla." (I almost fell off my chair.)"
- ¿Te caigo bien?" (Do you like me?)"
- ¡Ya caigo!" (Oh, I get it now!/I understand!)
- "A veces caigo, pero nunca me rindo." (I sometimes fall, but I never give up.)

### rendir

Rindo" is the first-person singular present indicative form of the Spanish verb `rendir` meaning    
"I produce," "I perform," "I yield," or "I pay" (homage).

It is also frequently used in the phrase

- no te rindas - don't give up
- "Me rindo," which means "I give up,"
- "I surrender," or "I quit".

Here are the primary meanings based on context:

- "I perform" / "I yield" (General/Work): No rindo bien por la mañana. (I don't perform well in the morning).
- "I produce" / "I yield" (Financial): Esta cuenta rinde 0.25% anual. (This account yields 0.25% annually).
- "I pay" (Homage/Tribute): Rindo homenaje a mis ancestros. (I pay homage to my ancestors)."I surrender" / - "I quit" (Reflexive - Me rindo): ¡Me rindo! ¡No puedo más! (I give up! I can't take any more!).
