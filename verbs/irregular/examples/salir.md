

"Salgo" is the first-person singular present tense conjugation of the Spanish verb *salir*, meaning "I leave" or "I go out." It is used for leaving a place, going out socially, or dating. \[[1](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=g99mD5xETZs&t=71), [2](https://www.spanishdict.com/translate/yo%20salgo)\]

Leaving/Going Out (General)

* Salgo de mi casa a las siete. (I leave my house at seven.)  
* Ahora salgo. (I'm going out now.)  
* Salgo temprano del trabajo. (I leave work early.)  
* Siempre salgo por la puerta trasera. (I always go out through the back door.)  
* Si llueve, esta noche no salgo. (If it rains, I won't go out tonight.) \[[1](https://www.spanishdict.com/phrases/salgo), [2](https://hinative.com/questions/24674164), [3](https://www.clozemaster.com/translate/spa-eng/salgo), [4](https://www.spanishdict.com/translate/yo%20salgo)\]

Socializing/Dating

* Yo salgo con mis amigos. (I go out with my friends.)  
* Salgo con ella desde hace un año. (I've been going out with her for a year.)  
* Salgo a comer con mis padres. (I go out to eat with my parents.) \[[1](https://www.spanishdict.com/phrases/salgo), [2](https://www.spanishdict.com/translate/yo%20salgo)\]

Other Meanings (Looks/Turns out) \[[1](https://www.spanishdict.com/translate/yo%20salgo)\]

* Yo salgo muy mal en las fotos. (I look/turn out very bad in photos.)  
* Siempre salgo bien en las pruebas. (I always do well \[turn out well\] on tests.) \[[1](https://www.spanishdict.com/translate/yo%20salgo), [2](https://www.123teachme.com/translated_sentences/sp/salgo)\]
