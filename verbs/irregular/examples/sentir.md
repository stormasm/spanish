  
In Spanish, siento comes from the verb *sentir* (to feel, to sense, to regret). It is used to express emotions, physical sensations, or apologies. \[[1](https://www.spanish.academy/blog/sentirse-vs-sentarse-me-siento-lo-siento-lo-que-siento/), [2](https://www.spanishdict.com/phrases/siento), [3](https://quillbot.com/blog/frequently-asked-questions/what-does-lo-siento-mean/), [4](https://www.youtube.com/shorts/Q889coGpZHI), [5](https://spanish.kwiziq.com/revision/grammar/sentir-vs-sentirse-to-feel)\]

1\. "Lo siento" (I'm sorry / I regret it) \[[1](https://quillbot.com/blog/frequently-asked-questions/what-does-lo-siento-mean/)\]

* Lo siento mucho por llegar tarde. *(I am so sorry for being late.)*  
* Lo siento mucho por tu pérdida. *(I am so sorry for your loss.)*  
* Siento haberte hecho esperar. *(I am sorry to have made you wait.)* \[[1](https://www.collinsdictionary.com/sentences/spanish/siento), [2](https://quillbot.com/blog/frequently-asked-questions/what-does-lo-siento-mean/), [3](https://spanish.kwiziq.com/revision/grammar/sentir-vs-sentirse-to-feel)\]

2\. "Siento \+ noun" (I feel \[something\] / I sense \[something\]) \[[1](https://spanish.kwiziq.com/revision/grammar/sentir-vs-sentirse-to-feel), [2](https://www.spanish.academy/blog/sentirse-vs-sentarse-me-siento-lo-siento-lo-que-siento/)\]

* Siento frío. *(I feel cold.)*  
* Siento un dolor extraño en el pecho. *(I feel a strange pain in my chest.)*  
* Siento que algo va a salir mal. *(I feel that something is going to go wrong.)* \[[1](https://www.reddit.com/r/learnspanish/comments/14zs88z/why_would_you_say_me_siento_instead_of_yo_siento/), [2](https://www.spanishdict.com/translate/yo%20siento), [3](https://www.spanishdict.com/translate/la%20siento)\]

3\. "Me siento" (I sit down or I feel \[emotion/state\]) \[[1](https://www.facebook.com/61563069701844/videos/me-siento-confused-spanish-loves-a-little-double-troubleme-siento-can-mean-i-sit/3367110933457881/), [2](https://www.spanishdict.com/phrases/siento)\]

*(Note: For reflexive emotions and conditions, use "me siento" instead of just "siento")*. \[[1](https://www.tiktok.com/@studiaspanish/video/7322507275090857248), [2](https://www.reddit.com/r/learnspanish/comments/14zs88z/why_would_you_say_me_siento_instead_of_yo_siento/)\]

* Me siento muy feliz hoy. *(I feel very happy today.)*  
* Me siento en la silla. *(I sit down in the chair.)*  
* Me siento cansada después del trabajo. *(I feel tired after work.)* \[[1](https://www.tiktok.com/@studiaspanish/video/7322507275090857248), [2](https://www.facebook.com/61563069701844/videos/me-siento-confused-spanish-loves-a-little-double-troubleme-siento-can-mean-i-sit/3367110933457881/), [3](https://spanish.kwiziq.com/revision/grammar/sentir-vs-sentirse-to-feel)\]
