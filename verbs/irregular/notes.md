
There are 9 primary irregular verbs in the Spanish present tense (yo form) that end in -go (known as yo-go verbs), though over 80 verbs fit this pattern when including derived verbs, such as rehacer or mantener.

- Caer (caigo - I fall)
- Decir (digo - I say/tell)
- Hacer (hago - I do/make)
- Oír (oigo - I hear)
- Poner (pongo - I put)
- Salir (salgo - I leave/go out)
- Tener (tengo - I have)
- Traer (traigo - I bring)
- Valer (valgo - I am worth)
- Venir (vengo - I come)

Note: While some lists vary slightly (e.g., mentions only 9-10), the 9-10 listed above are the most common foundation.

The Spanish Academy blog states nearly 80 verbs belong to this category, but they are all derived from these core base verbs.

Key Takeaways:

-go verbs only include verbs ending in -er or -ir; there are no -ar verbs in this category.

They are irregular only in the "yo" (I) form; the rest of the conjugation generally follows normal rules, though many (like tener and venir) also have stem changes in other forms.

Ir (to go) is not a -go verb, as its conjugation is voy.
