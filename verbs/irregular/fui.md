
#### google: spanish examples of the verb fui with ser

In Spanish, fui is the first-person singular preterite (past tense) conjugation for both *ser* (to be) and *ir* (to go), meaning it can mean "I was" or "I went" depending on context. When using *fui* with *ser*, it often describes a profession, identity, or characteristic completed in the past. \[[1](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=f29kQThccEw), [2](https://www.youtube.com/shorts/oYwKpj3QqZg)\]

Examples of "Fui" (Ser \- "I was")

* Yo fui estudiante en la universidad. (I was a student at the university.)  
* Yo fui el mejor estudiante de la clase. (I was the best student in the class.)  
* Yo fui un miembro del Opus Dei durante siete años. (I was a member of Opus Dei for seven years.)  
* Fui maestro por dos años. (I was a teacher for two years.)  
* No fui muy inteligente. (I was not very intelligent.)  
* Fui invitado a dar varias conferencias. (I was invited to give several lectures.) \[[1](https://www.thoughtco.com/conjugation-of-ser-3079641), [2](https://migaku.com/blog/spanish/ser-preterite-conjugation-spanish-guide), [3](https://www.spanishdict.com/conjugate/fui), [4](https://www.123teachme.com/learn_spanish/preterite_ser), [5](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=f29kQThccEw)\]

How to Differentiate *Ser* from *Ir*

* Ser (Was): Usually followed by a noun or adjective (e.g., *fui estudiante*).  
* Ir (Went): Usually followed by the preposition "a" or "para" (e.g., *fui a la playa* \- I went to the beach). \[[1](https://www.reddit.com/r/learnspanish/comments/1ap1rl4/does_ir_and_ser_conjugate_the_same_in_past_tense/), [2](https://www.facebook.com/groups/spanishtips2024/posts/8942435792474860/), [3](https://www.spanish.academy/blog/all-you-ever-needed-to-know-about-spanish-simple-past-tense-verbs/), [4](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=f29kQThccEw)\]
