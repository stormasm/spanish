### volver vs devolver

Volver means to return to a place or do something again (come back), while devolver means to give something back to someone (return an object) or, less commonly, to throw up. The key difference is that volver involves returning oneself or an action, whereas devolver involves returning a specific item or concept to its original owner or location, often something borrowed or received.

##### Volver (to return/come back/do again)

- Returning to a place: Vuelvo a casa. (I'm returning home.)
- Repeating an action: Volví a leer el libro. (I read the book again.)
- Related to "turning": Think of it as "re-turning" or turning back.

##### Devolver (to give back/return an item)

- Returning an object: Devuelve el libro a la biblioteca. (Return the book to the library.)
- Giving something back: Devuélveme mis llaves. (Give me my keys back.)
- Medical meaning: Devolver can also mean to throw up (vomit).

##### Key Distinction

- Volver: Focuses on the subject returning or repeating.
- Devolver: Focuses on returning an object to its source.

Example:
- Yo vuelvo a mi casa. (I'm coming back home.)
- Yo devuelvo el libro a la biblioteca. (I'm returning the book to the library.)
