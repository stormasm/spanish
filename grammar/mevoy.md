


The primary difference between voy and me voy in Spanish is that voy (from ir) indicates "I am going" to a destination, while me voy (from irse) means "I am leaving" or "I am going away" from a place.

Here is a detailed breakdown of the differences:
1. Voy (I am going)
Used to state where you are going or what you are going to do. It focuses on the action of moving forward to a destination.
Voy al supermercado. (I am going to the supermarket.)
Voy a estudiar. (I am going to study.)
2. Me voy (I am leaving/I'm off)
Used to indicate that you are leaving your current location. It often implies a farewell or a change of location.
Me voy a casa. (I'm leaving/going home.)
¡Ya me voy! (I'm leaving now! / I'm off!)
3. Key Scenarios & Nuances
Leaving vs. Going: "Voy a la fiesta" means you are headed to a party (but might not be there yet). "Me voy de la fiesta" means you are currently leaving the party.
"Me voy a..." (Leaving for a destination): You can use me voy a to say you are leaving the current place to go to another.
Example: Me voy a España. (I am leaving [here] for Spain/I'm off to Spain.)
Urgency: Adding "ya" (already/now) to me voy increases the urgency of leaving.
Example: Ya me voy. (I'm leaving right now/I'm out).
"Ya voy" (I'm coming/coming!): Note that "Ya voy" is often used to mean "I'm coming/I'll be there in a moment," different from "Ya me voy" (I'm leaving).

Comparison Table
Spanish	Meaning	Focus
Voy	I'm going	Destination/Action

Summary Example
Voy a la playa: I'm going to the beach (eventually).
Me voy a la playa: I'm leaving [now] to go to the beach.
