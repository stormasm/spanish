
##### Difference between pasear and caminar

Caminar means to walk. Move your body by putting one leg in front of the other. The word caminar just focus on the movement.

Pasear is quite different. When you use the word pasear you are focusing on the journey. It generally implies a pleasant experience. Besides, you can “pasear” walking, biking, horse-riding, driving. Alone or with others. Enjoying the surroundings.

### Bugambilia

Yes, buganvilia and bugambilia are the same plant, referring to the popular, thorny, flowering ornamental vine technically known as Bougainvillea.

- Key Details:
- Bugambilia: This spelling is common in Mexico, Guatemala, Cuba, and the Philippines.
- Buganvilia: This spelling is very common in Portuguese (often buganvília) and some Spanish-speaking regions.
- Commonality: Both are commonly used, but they represent the same plant, known for its vibrant bracts (often mistaken for flowers) in colors like pink, purple, red, and yellow.
Other Names: The plant is known by many names globally, including Veranera, Trinitaria, Santa Rita, and Papelillo.

Bougainvillea is the formal botanical name, while "bugambilia" and "buganvilia" are the popular, everyday names used in Spanish and Portuguese, respectively.
